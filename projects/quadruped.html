Overview

A purely mechanical quadruped platform designed to study power transmission, torque amplification, and actuation strategies in legged robots. Unlike typical servo-based quadrupeds, this system employs a hybrid actuation architecture to achieve higher torque density, improved efficiency, and better dynamic performance.

The robot features a custom-designed leg drivetrain focused on robustness and precision:

  • Primary Leg Drive: Actuated by BLDC Motors using a Timing Belt & Pulley system to reduce backlash, handle higher loads and improved torque-speed tradeoff.
  • Position Feedback: Integrated Absolute Encoders at the output shaft (the joint) for precise position control without homing.
  • Hip & Thigh: Actuated by high-torque servos for multi-degree-of-freedom movement.
